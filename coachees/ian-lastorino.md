## Open threads / revisit next session
- **Confirmed (2026-06-17):** Ian *did* assume directs "should already know" at their level (decades of experience). This assumption has now been reframed: it's not about craft or competence—it's a **context gap**. Ian holds specific knowledge of stakeholder needs, constraints, and priorities that his directs have never been given. This context doesn't transfer through seniority; it transfers through explicit communication.
- **The reframe:** The issue isn't "they should know" (unfair assumption about directs) → it's "I have information they don't" (clear lever for Ian). His directs deliver technically sound work that misses because they're aiming at "good in general" while Ian is aiming at "what stakeholders will accept." Two different targets.
- **Experiment (delegation/feedback):** In the next delegation cycle, explicitly share stakeholder context: who cares about this work, what do they want, what are the real constraints? Observe whether deliverables land closer and frustration decreases.
- **Next move (delegation/feedback):** After testing, explore *why* Ian wasn't sharing context before — was it time, or assumption that explaining would seem condescending to experienced directs? That belief is where the longer-term shift lives.
