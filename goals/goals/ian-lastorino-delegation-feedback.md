# Improve delegation and feedback with direct reports

## Goal statement
Ian is caught in a cycle: he gives direction/feedback to direct reports, their deliverables don't match, and he ends up redoing the work himself. The goal is to understand the root cause of this pattern and shift from "fix it myself" to "communicate effectively so it lands the first time."

## Status history
- **2026-06-17 — opened** at intake. Initial signal: direct reports aren't meeting direction; Ian reacts by redoing work rather than iterating.
- **2026-06-17 — root cause mapped** after deep-dive session. The feedback-landing gap is now specific: Ian gives the what (correction) but not the why (standard/intent). Ready to test explicit explanation of rationale in next delegation cycle.
- **2026-06-17 — assumption confirmed and reframed** after stakeholder-context session. Ian confirms he assumed directs "should already know" the standard. Coach reframes: this is a **context gap, not a competence gap**. Ian holds stakeholder knowledge his directs don't have. Next step: test explicitly sharing stakeholder context (who cares, what they want, constraints) in next delegation cycle and measure whether deliverables land closer and frustration decreases.
