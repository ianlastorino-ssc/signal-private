# 2026-06-17 — Retention: naming what you see vs. asking if everything's okay

## Context

Continuation of the Charles retention thread. The fade-vs-flip diagnostic from prior
sessions was still open. This exchange moved past the diagnostic into what Ian has
actually done — and what's missing.

## What happened

Ian answered the fade-vs-flip question: **"I don't know."** He can't tell whether it's
been building or something specific triggered it — which turns out to be the real signal:
he hasn't had the conversation that would tell him.

Ian then revealed what he *had* done: he'd asked Charles "is everything alright?" and got
"yeah, I'm just busy." He took that at face value.

The coach used a perspective-taking move — put yourself in Charles's seat, manager asks
"everything alright?" what do you say? Ian answered immediately: "Fine." He saw it
himself: the question writes its own deflecting reply.

Coach named the reframe: a vague "are you okay?" is easy to wave off. What's hard to
wave off is when you name specifically what you've noticed and then go quiet. The
conversation is no longer about feelings in the abstract — it's about observable facts
the person can't dismiss.

Session ended with an open question to Ian: if you sat down with Charles deliberately —
not in the hallway — what specific things would you point to?

## Open thread

Ian has not yet answered what he'd name to Charles. That's the next move: getting him to
articulate the specific observations he'd surface, because those are what make the
conversation real rather than deflectable.
