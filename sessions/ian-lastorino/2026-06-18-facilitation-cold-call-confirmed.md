# 2026-06-18 — Facilitation: cold-call confirmed, perspective-taking question in flight

## Exchange

Coach's prior open diagnostic: were activities structured with pairs/small groups before
whole-room share, or was Ian cold-calling the full room directly?

Ian answers: "Speak cold." No warm-up structure. The first ask was to speak out loud,
in front of the whole room, from a standing start.

Coach names what that means structurally: the very first move was to ask participants to
perform for the room with no low-stakes on-ramp. Then pivots to a perspective-taking
question: put yourself in that seat — you have a half-formed thought, the facilitator is
scanning the room. What makes you actually say it versus sit on it?

## Signal

- **Structural gap confirmed:** Ian asked participants to speak cold to the whole room.
  No pairs, no small groups, no low-stakes rehearsal before the public share. This is the
  design mechanism behind the social gap already confirmed: people had content and wouldn't
  volunteer it. The format removed the on-ramp.
- **Perspective-taking prompt in flight:** Coach's question asks Ian to inhabit the
  participant experience. His answer will reveal whether he already has a felt sense of the
  social risk, or whether the on-ramp function is still abstract to him — and will shape
  whether the next move is design (add the structure) or empathy (understand why it matters).
