# 2026-06-18 — Training facilitation: defending the whole-room format choice

## Exchange

Ian revisits the earlier participation diagnostic. He pushes back on the implicit small-groups
frame: he knows small groups loosen things up, but this wasn't "that type of training" — it
was a collaborative session, and the whole-room format was a deliberate choice.

Coach accepts the distinction and pivots the diagnostic: since the whole-room format was
intentional, what was it supposed to give the session that breaking into groups would have
cost? The question probes whether the format choice was grounded in a specific outcome Ian
was protecting, or whether "collaborative session" is a category label that naturalizes
whole-room without fully accounting for the tradeoff.

## Signal

- **Format was deliberate, not default:** Ian wasn't unaware of small-group benefits — he
  named them first. The whole-room choice was a conscious call based on how he categorized
  the session.
- **Session taxonomy is doing real work:** Ian draws a distinction between "training" sessions
  and "collaborative sessions," and treats that distinction as load-bearing for format decisions.
  The coach is now probing whether that taxonomy maps cleanly onto what generates participation,
  or whether it's a mental model that can produce unexamined tradeoffs.
- **Diagnostic shift:** The earlier cognitive/readiness vs. social/safety frame was predicated
  on design being sound. Ian's defense of whole-room opens a different angle: the format
  itself may have shaped the silence, regardless of whether participants were ready or
  reluctant. Coach's question is in flight — Ian hasn't yet named what whole-room was
  protecting.
