# 2026-06-17 — Stuck in role: direction unknown, pivoting to past positive experiences

## What happened

Ian responded to the six-months-out question ("Picture a version of this six months out
where you don't feel stuck — what's actually different in it?") with: "Thats the thing,
I dont know, think you can help?"

This confirms the coach's read from the prior session: the issue isn't about a specific
ambition being blocked or a specific dimension of work being wrong — it's that Ian
doesn't yet have a clear picture of what he wants at all.

The coach reframed not knowing as a legitimate starting point rather than a deficiency:
nobody figures this out by staring at a blank future and trying to invent a plan. The
pivot: look backward at the last stretch where work didn't feel like this — a time Ian
felt like he was actually moving, or genuinely into what he was doing — and surface what
was true about it.

Session ended at the coach's question: "Take me back to the last stretch where work
didn't feel like this. What were you working on?"

## Signals

- Ian confirmed he has no clear vision of where he'd want his career or role to go.
  He can name that he's stuck; he cannot yet name what "unstuck" looks like.
- This rules out "blocked ambition" as the primary frame — if he knew what he wanted
  and was being blocked, he'd have named it. The gap is direction itself.
- Willingness to engage: "I don't know, think you can help?" is an opening, not a
  deflection. Ian is ready to work on this.

## Open thread

Session ended at: "Take me back to the last stretch where work didn't feel like this.
What were you working on?" Resume here — his answer will surface what kinds of work or
conditions actually engage him, which is the raw material for figuring out direction.
