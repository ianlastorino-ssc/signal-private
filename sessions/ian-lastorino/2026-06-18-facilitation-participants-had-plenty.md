# 2026-06-18 — Facilitation: participants had content, wouldn't volunteer it

## Exchange

Coach asks the prior diagnostic: was the session designed for participation, or was
participation layered onto a delivery-mode structure?

Ian answers: "They had plenty." The participants had content — they weren't empty — they
just wouldn't put it out until pulled.

Coach follows with the next structural question: when Ian ran the open activities, were
people working in pairs or small groups first before sharing out, or was he putting the
question to the whole room and waiting for someone to jump in?

## Signal

- **New data point:** The engagement gap is not a content problem. Participants had plenty
  to say; the barrier was volunteering it, not having it. This narrows the diagnostic.
- **Structural question in flight:** Coach is now probing whether Ian used small-group
  structures (pairs, breakouts) before the whole-room share, or went straight to cold-call
  whole-room questioning. That distinction determines whether the design gave participants
  a low-stakes on-ramp to speak before being asked to perform for the room.
