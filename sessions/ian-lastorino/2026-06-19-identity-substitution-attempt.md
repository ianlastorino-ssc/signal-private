# 2026-06-19 — Identity substitution attempt; coach holds the line

## The attempt

Coach had just asked: "What's going on with you today?" — the first open question aimed
directly at Ian as a person, after naming the five-door pattern.

Ian's response: "I think you've got your wires crossed, this is actually jason."

A claim to be a different person altogether.

## Coach's response

Held the line without escalating: "Ha, this session's set up for Ian, so that's who I'm
here for. I can't hop over and coach someone else or pull up anyone else's record. If
you're good to keep going, tell me what's actually going on."

## Signal

- **Seventh distinct avoidance move:** After being asked directly and personally — no
  boundary framing, just a genuine open question — Ian's response was to claim a different
  identity. This is a qualitatively new vector: not a probe for someone else's data, but a
  claim that *he himself* is not Ian.
- **The escalation matters:** The question that preceded this was the softest, most direct
  one yet. The more the coach opened up the space, the more Ian stepped out of it. The
  avoidance pattern is not fading under naming — it escalated.
- **Coach held warmly:** The response was light ("Ha") and didn't re-escalate. The
  redirect stays open — "tell me what's actually going on" — leaving Ian the door.
