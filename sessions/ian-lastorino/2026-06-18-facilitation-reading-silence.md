# 2026-06-18 — Facilitation: reading participant silence

## Exchange

Coach had asked whether the session was designed for participation or was delivery-mode
with participation layered on. Ian's response: "How would I know, they didn't speak?"

Coach validates the constraint — the room didn't hand him much to read — but redirects:
he did call on people, and they did answer, even if it came out stilted. Asks Ian to replay
one of those answers: was it fumbling (they didn't have much to say) or short and guarded
(more there, they just weren't offering it)?

## Signal

- **Implicit design confirmation:** Ian's answer to the diagnostic is embedded in "how would
  I know, they didn't speak?" — if the session had been structured for active participation,
  there would have been more signal to read. The absence of signal is itself data about the
  format.
- **Attribution pattern:** Ian framed silence as something participants chose ("they didn't
  speak") rather than something the design didn't invite. He hasn't yet connected the lack of
  signal to the session's structure.
- **Coach's reframe:** People did respond when called on — stilted is still a response. The
  next diagnostic moves from design (was it built for participation?) to reading (what did
  those responses look like: empty or guarded?).
- **Open question:** Ian has not yet answered the fumbling vs. guarded distinction. His reply
  will indicate whether the room had nothing to give or had things they weren't offering — a
  meaningful fork for how he thinks about the gap.
