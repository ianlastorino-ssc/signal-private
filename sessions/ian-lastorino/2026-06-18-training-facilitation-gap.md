# 2026-06-18 — Training facilitation: the engagement gap

## Exchange

Ian describes a training session he ran today. The audience was flat and unresponsive in
the room — he called on people to get interaction but it felt stilted. Afterwards, attendees
told him they found it valuable.

Coach names the gap: flat in the room, but they walk out saying it landed. Asks a diagnostic
question before going further: was the session designed for participation — structured for
people to work a problem, talk to each other, react to something — or was it primarily
delivery and Ian reached for participation as he went?

## Signal

- **The gap:** Ian noticed a split between in-room engagement (low, stilted) and post-session
  perceived value (positive). He can't account for it.
- **Facilitation move attempted:** He called on people to drive interaction. His own read:
  it felt stilted — the technique wasn't landing.
- **Design question unresolved:** Coach's diagnostic is in flight. Ian has not yet answered
  whether the session was architected for participation or whether participation was layered
  onto a delivery-mode session after the fact.
- **Notable context:** Ian is Head of L&D — facilitation design is his professional domain.
  The fact that this session created a gap he can't explain is itself worth sitting with.
