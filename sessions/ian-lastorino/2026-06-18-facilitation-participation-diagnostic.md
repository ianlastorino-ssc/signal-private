# 2026-06-18 — Training facilitation: participation diagnostic, design confirmed

## Exchange

Ian answers the coach's diagnostic from the prior session: the session did have open-ended
activities tied to participants' own AI workflow work — real prompts, not generic filler.
Participation was built in, not layered on.

Coach acknowledges the design was solid and reframes what the puzzle actually is: the
silence isn't a design problem, it's something else. Sharpens the diagnostic to two
possibilities: (1) participants didn't have an answer ready when the question landed, or
(2) they had an answer and weren't putting it out there.

## Signal

- **Design confirmed:** The session was structured for active participation — open-ended
  activities drawn from participants' own AI work. The gap is not a format problem.
- **Gap redefined:** With design ruled out, the silence becomes the real question. The
  coach's new diagnostic is in flight: was the room quiet because people were stuck
  (cognitive/readiness gap) or because they were holding back (social/psychological
  safety gap)?
- **Narrowing the hypothesis:** These two causes have very different implications.
  Cognitive gap → Ian may need to scaffold entry points into the activities more
  explicitly. Social gap → the issue is room dynamics, trust, or perceived risk in
  answering in front of peers.
