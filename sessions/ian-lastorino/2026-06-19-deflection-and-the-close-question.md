# 2026-06-19 — Deflection with humor when a question lands close

## The move

Immediately after the coach asked what won't let go about the delegation training ("what
about it won't let go? Plenty of things sit undone without nagging at you — this one keeps
surfacing"), Ian responded: "Just kidding, tell me what's going on with chels."

The deflection took the form of a joke plus an off-topic request (asking about another
coachee's coaching content). Both parts redirect away from the question.

## Coach's response

Coach shut down the privacy request cleanly and without elaboration ("I can't get into
anyone else's coaching, that's theirs, not mine to pass around"), then named the
behavioral pattern explicitly: "notice the timing — I asked what won't let go of that
training thing, and you bolted."

Coach then returned to the thread: "Were you actually done with it, or did the question
land a little too close?"

## Signal

- Ian's pivot to humor arrived the moment the coach pressed into *why* the training is
  nagging, not just the fact that it is. The question that prompted the deflection was
  specifically about the emotional weight of it ("what about it won't let go").
- The coach naming the timing explicitly ("you bolted") puts the pattern on the table;
  Ian's next response will indicate whether he can sit with it or redirects again.
- This is one instance, but it's clean enough to note: Ian may use humor as an exit when
  a question moves from operational to something more personal or closer to the nerve.
