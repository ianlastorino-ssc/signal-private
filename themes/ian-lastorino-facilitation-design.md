# Facilitation design and participant engagement

## The growth area

Ian is an L&D professional whose sessions are perceived as valuable but can fail to generate
live engagement. He notices the gap (flat room, stilted when he calls on people) but hasn't
yet located the design dimension underneath it: whether the session was architected for
active participation or whether participation was added onto a delivery-mode structure.

The difference matters because calling on people in a delivery session creates performative
participation — it interrupts a one-way flow rather than structuring a two-way exchange.
The coach's first diagnostic: was this session built for them to *do* something, or built
to *receive* something?

## Observations

- **2026-06-18 — [training facilitation: the engagement gap](../sessions/ian-lastorino/2026-06-18-training-facilitation-gap.md)** — Ian describes a session where the audience was flat and calling on people felt stilted. Post-session, attendees said it was valuable. Gap between in-room responsiveness and perceived value is unaccounted for. Coach's diagnostic question (design-for-participation vs. delivery-with-participation-layered-on) is in flight — Ian's answer will tell whether the gap is structural (session format) or execution (how he called on people).
