# Managing up and upward influence

## The opening

Ian surfaces this area himself: he's receiving direction from above for his department — tasks and priorities delegated down — with limited direct say in shaping them. He wants to know how to engage when he doesn't agree with the direction.

## What Ian wants

He wants to actually *change* the direction, not just execute through disagreement. This is a growth signal: he's not asking how to cope or stay sane; he's asking how to actually move something.

## Coaching framework surfaced (2026-06-17)

The key reframe from the opening session: what moves decisions from above is **information they don't have**, not another opinion. Leadership already weighed opinions and made a call; adding another view is friction, not movement. The leverage Ian has is his unique vantage point — ground-level visibility that leadership can't have from their altitude.

The critical diagnostic: Is Ian holding *new information* (a cost, a risk, a way the direction breaks on the ground)? Or would he weigh the *same facts* differently? The first is a winnable influence play; the second is a values difference — almost never moved by argument, and trying spends capital on an unwinnable case.
