# AI adoption and personal fluency

## The situation
The org is actively pushing AI adoption. Ian, as the L&D leader, is positioned both as a personal user and as the person responsible for bringing AI capability to everyone else. He self-identifies as not knowing where to start — the pressure is real but the entry point isn't clear.

## Observations
- **2026-06-17 — [AI adoption: blank page, delegation training, and prompting](../sessions/ian-lastorino/2026-06-17-ai-adoption-opening.md)** — Ian surfaces this topic unprompted, redirecting from delegation/feedback work. Sequences correctly: get fluent personally before building training for the org. Identifies his primary content slog as blank-page starts — not production/formatting. Takes well to the "wall to bounce off" framing: AI's job is to give you something to react to, not to be right. His first test case is a delegation training for new managers — the same topic he's been working through personally. First prompting attempt ("I need a delegation training to teach new managers how to properly delegate tasks...") is all topic, no problem context — every word applies to every company. Coach names the pattern: he described the *what*, not the specific failure mode he's actually seen. Ian hasn't yet supplied the situational texture that would make a prompt produce something worth reacting to. Session ends with Ian asking for more prompting guidance — he's oriented toward skill-building, not resistant.
