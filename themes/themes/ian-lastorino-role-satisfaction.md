# Role satisfaction and career growth

## The opening

Ian raised this unprompted at the start of a new session: "I feel stuck in my role."
This is the first signal that his experience of the role itself — beyond managing his team
effectively — is something he wants to work on.

The coach posed a clarifying either/or: is it about advancement (nowhere to go from here)
or about day-to-day meaning (the work has stopped mattering)? Ian had not answered when
that session ended.

In the next exchange, Ian answered: both. "Every day is the same, it isn't going anywhere."
The coach connected the two: when both are true simultaneously, it usually signals an
absence of clear direction — no picture of where he actually wants this to go. The coach
asked him to imagine a six-months-out version where he doesn't feel stuck, and what's
different in it.

Ian answered: "Thats the thing, I dont know." He has no clear picture of what unstuck
would look like — which rules out "blocked ambition" as the frame. If he knew what he
wanted and was being blocked, he'd have named it. The gap is direction itself. The coach
reframed not knowing as a starting point rather than a deficiency and pivoted to asking
about the last stretch where work actually felt engaging.
